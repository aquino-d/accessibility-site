Non-Cognitive Accessibility Recommedations

Discover a few recommendations that can be use to help our community

  • Complete Blindess

    Defined: A user who is using a screen reader to access websites. Creating Accessibility: Logical Flow, descending headers, skipable navigation, use tables with summaries. Also keep in mind: Graphs and diagrams with conclusions, forms with labels. Don't forget about: CAPTCHA and being able to use a site without JavaScript.

  • Visual Accessibility (Low Vision and Color Blindness)

    Defined: Low Vision users include those who must adjust settings for the monitor and Color Blind users will include individuals who can not tell colors apart to those who can see no color at all. Creating Accessibility: Proper color contrast is paramount for those who need Visual Accessibility.

  • Audio Accessibility

    Defined: Users who can't hear, are partially deaf, or wear hearing aids. Creating Accessibility: Use effective subtitles on Videos and include live chat.

  • Physical Accessibility

    Defined: Includes anyone who might have trouble with traditional computer input methods. Creating Accessibility: Use One-field-per-line forms and avoid popup / timing issues.
